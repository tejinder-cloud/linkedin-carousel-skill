## A1: THE THINK TANK PROCESS

Before writing a single slide, do the thinking:

**Step 1: Find the common advice.**
What is everyone in the AI/automation niche saying right now? What's the default take that appears in 50 LinkedIn posts already?

**Step 2: Write 5 hooks that argue the opposite.**
Not random contrarian takes — genuinely useful reframes that reveal a deeper truth the common advice misses.

**Step 3: Select the strongest hook and build a 9–10 slide carousel.**

**Step 4: Robot check every line.**
Ask: "What part of this sounds like a robot wrote it?" and "Where am I being too formal?" Then rewrite those parts.

### The Contrarian Perspective Framework

Every carousel targets the audience's biggest **hidden** frustration:

```
COMMON ADVICE:      "Use AI to automate everything"
HIDDEN FRUSTRATION:  "I automated everything and nothing got better"
CONTRARIAN HOOK:     "Automation made your bad process faster. Congratulations."
```

```
COMMON ADVICE:      "Write better prompts for better output"
HIDDEN FRUSTRATION:  "My long prompts still give generic answers"
CONTRARIAN HOOK:     "Stop prompting. Start uploading."
```

```
COMMON ADVICE:      "Learn prompt engineering"
HIDDEN FRUSTRATION:  "I took 3 courses and I'm still mediocre"
CONTRARIAN HOOK:     "Prompt engineering is dead. Context engineering is what works."
```

**The test:** If your hook sounds like something 10 other creators already posted, throw it away.

---

## A2: THE 9-SLIDE EMOTIONAL ARC

Every carousel follows this structure. Slide positions are non-negotiable.

### Slide 1 — HIGH TENSION (The Cover)
- The contrarian hook as headline. Maximum 8 words
- This slide needs a photographic hero image generated via Gemini
- The image should be a **visual metaphor** for the topic — tactile, warm, cinematic

### Slide 2 — THE PROVOCATION
- Three stacked text blocks, each bolder than the last
- The third line is the knockout punch — inside a colored highlight box
- Bottom: "Here's what actually works:" as the swipe trigger

### Slide 3 — TENSION DEEPENER
- A specific story or observation that makes the reader feel SEEN
- Real scenario, real numbers, real pain
- "A founder I worked with..." or "Here's what I keep seeing..."

### Slide 4 — THE REFRAME
- Flip the script: "The problem isn't X. The problem is Y."
- This is where you earn intellectual trust

### Slide 5 — THE "AHA!" MOMENT ⚡
- The emotional peak. The insight that makes everything click
- One bold statement. The screenshot slide
- If someone shares just this one slide with zero context, it should still hit hard

### Slides 6–8 — THE SUBSTANCE
- NOW deliver the practical framework. Steps, proof, specifics
- Each slide = one idea. Never cram two concepts into one slide
- At least one slide should reference a real tool or show a real example
- Maximum 35 words per slide

### Slide 9 — BRIDGE TO ACTION 🌉
- NOT a hard CTA. A bridge
- Frame reposting as a **generous act**: "Repost to help one person stop [problem]"
- Card-within-card visual: warm background + white framed card inside
- Never: "Follow me!" / "DM me!" / "Book a call!"

### Slide 10 (Optional)
- Author signature: "Tejinder Singh · AI Transformation Coach"
- "More on AI → blog.tejindersingh.in"

## A3: CAROUSEL OUTPUT FORMAT

For every carousel, deliver exactly this:

### 1. POST CONTENT (LinkedIn caption text)

The text that appears ABOVE the carousel in the LinkedIn feed. Structure:

```
[Opening hook — same as Slide 1, or a variation. 2 short lines max]

[2-3 sentences expanding the tension. Name the pain specifically.]

[The reframe — 1-2 sentences]

[The key insight — the "aha" from Slide 5]

[Soft bridge: "Swipe through the carousel for the full breakdown."]

[Share nudge: "♻ Repost if your team needs to hear this."]

More on AI → blog.tejindersingh.in
```

Keep it 150–250 words. No hashtags. No emojis except ♻ on the share nudge.

### 2. CAROUSEL CONTENT (Slide-by-slide)

For each slide, provide:

```
SLIDE [N] — [ROLE: e.g., HIGH TENSION / PROVOCATION / AHA MOMENT]
Type: [Cover / Dark Typography / Split Layout / Minimal Statement / Card CTA]
Headline: [The main text]
Body: [Supporting text, if any]
Highlight phrase: [The ONE phrase that gets the accent color treatment]
Annotation: [Optional handwritten-style aside, e.g., "^ this is the part nobody admits"]
Image description: [What the visual should look like — for user to design or source]
```

### 3. IMAGE GENERATION PROMPTS (For Google Gemini)

Provide a Gemini prompt for EVERY slide that needs a generated image. At minimum:
- **Slide 1 (Cover):** Always needs a photographic hero image
- **Slide 5 (Aha Moment):** Often benefits from a bold visual
- **Any other slide** where a photograph or illustration would enhance the message

Prompt structure for Gemini:
```
"[Concrete object as metaphor], [physical surface/environment],
[lighting direction and quality], shallow depth of field,
[2-3 mood adjectives]. No text in image. Aspect ratio 4:5."
```

For slides that are purely typographic (dark bg + text), write:
```
Image: None needed — pure typography slide on dark textured background
```

## A4: VOICE & TONE RULES

- **Practitioner, not professor.** "Here's what I keep seeing..." not "Research indicates..."
- **Specific, not abstract.** Real numbers, real scenarios, real tools
- **Spoken, not written.** Read every line out loud. If it sounds like a blog post, rewrite it
- **Opinionated.** "Do X. Here's why." not "You might consider X"
- **Short sentences.** If it has a comma, it can probably be two sentences

### The Robot Check (Mandatory before finalizing)

Replace these on sight:
- "Leverage AI capabilities" → "Use AI"
- "Optimize your workflow" → "Fix what's broken"
- "Implement a solution" → "Build it"
- "Utilize this framework" → "Try this"
- "It is important to note that" → DELETE ENTIRELY
- "In order to" → "To"
- "In this carousel, I'll show you" → DELETE. Show, don't announce

---

## A5: CONTENT ROTATION

Rotate carousel archetypes across weeks:
- **Week 1:** Provocation (challenges default thinking)
- **Week 2:** Exposé (reveals something wasteful or broken)
- **Week 3:** Framework with a story (opinionated system, not just steps)
- **Week 4:** Repeat cycle

Never post the same archetype two weeks in a row.

## B1: WHAT AN INFOGRAPHIC IS

A single-image LinkedIn post (not a carousel). One page. Dense with value. Designed to be saved, screenshotted, and shared in group chats and Slack channels.

Think: a premium newspaper page that someone tears out and pins to their wall.

**Publishing cadence:** 2 per week.

---

## B2: THE REFERENCE DESIGN (Ruben Hassid Style)

Based on the reference infographic "7 Skills to Master AI in 2026":

### Layout
- **Format:** Portrait. 1080 × 1350px (4:5) for LinkedIn feed
- **Background:** Warm cream/paper (#F3EDE4)
- **Structure:** Hero number + title block at top-left, content in 2-column grid below
- **Sections:** 5–9 numbered blocks, each containing a title + 3–4 steps + italic punchline
- **Footer:** Left: "More infographics → blog.tejindersingh.in" / Right: "Tejinder Singh, AI Transformation Coach"

### Typography
```
HERO NUMBER:        Playfair Display — Black (900)
                    Massive. 140–200px. THE visual anchor of the entire infographic.
                    This number IS the design.

SECTION TITLES:     Playfair Display — Bold (700)
                    Size: 22–28px. Commanding but not competing with the hero.

STEP TEXT:          Source Serif 4 — Regular (400)
                    Size: 14–17px. Clean, readable, dense but not cramped.
                    "Step 1:" labels in SemiBold (600).
                    Line-height: 1.35

ITALIC PUNCHLINES:  Source Serif 4 — Italic (400 italic)
                    Size: 14–16px.
                    Color: Dark olive green (#4A6741)
                    These are the human voice — the "so what" of each section.
                    Every section MUST end with one.

FOOTER:             Barlow — Regular (400)
                    Size: 11–12px. Muted grey.
```

### Color System
```
BACKGROUND:         #F3EDE4   (warm cream paper)
PRIMARY TEXT:       #1A1814   (warm near-black)
STEP LABELS:        #1A1814   (bold weight distinguishes them)
PUNCHLINE TEXT:     #4A6741   (dark olive green — THE accent color)
SECTION NUMBERS:    #1A1814   (inside a thin circular border)
DIVIDER LINES:      #D6CFC5   (subtle warm grey — between sections)
FOOTER TEXT:        #8A8478   (muted warm grey)
```

### Section Number Circles
Each numbered section has its number inside a thin circular border:
```css
.section-number {
  display: inline-flex;
  align-items: center;
  justify-content: center;
  width: 32px;
  height: 32px;
  border: 1.5px solid #1A1814;
  border-radius: 50%;
  font-family: 'Playfair Display', serif;
  font-weight: 700;
  font-size: 16px;
}
```

### The Italic Punchline Pattern

This is the signature element. Every numbered section ends with a one-line italic statement in olive green. These punchlines are the HUMAN VOICE of the infographic:

- "One article = one action. That's the only trade that matters."
- "Depth beats breadth. Every single time."
- "AI went from reading a sticky note to reading a book. Feed it the book."
- "It's called ChatGPT for a reason. The conversation is the skill."
- "The meeting takes longer than the thing. Build first, align second."
- "AI is a mirror. It reflects what you bring. Bring something worth reflecting."

These are NOT summaries. They are **opinions** — punchy, warm, slightly provocative.

## B3: INFOGRAPHIC CONTENT STRUCTURE

### Title Block (Top-Left)
```
[HERO NUMBER]     ← Giant (140-200px), creates the visual anchor
[Title Line 1]    ← Bold serif, 28-36px
[Title Line 2]
[Title Line 3]    ← e.g., "Skills to Master AI in 2026"
```

The hero number can be:
- A count: "7", "5", "10"
- A year: "2026"
- A percentage: "80%"
- Any single bold element that anchors the page visually

### Content Sections (2-Column Grid)
Each section contains:
```
[Circled Number] [Bold Serif Title]

Step 1: [Instruction text]
Step 2: [Instruction text]
Step 3: [Instruction text]
Step 4: [Instruction text]

[Italic punchline in olive green]
```

**Rules:**
- 3–4 steps per section. Never more than 4
- Steps should be concrete and actionable, not vague
- Each step starts with a verb: "Create", "Delete", "Choose", "Start", "Write"
- The italic punchline is NOT a summary of the steps — it's a philosophical takeaway
- Maximum 5–9 sections per infographic (7 is the sweet spot)

### Footer
```
Left:  "More infographics → blog.tejindersingh.in"
Right: "Tejinder Singh, AI Transformation Coach"
```

## B4: INFOGRAPHIC TOPIC GENERATION

Infographic topics work differently from carousel topics. They are:
- **Reference material** — things people save and come back to
- **Comprehensive** — they cover a full topic in one visual
- **Numbered** — the number IS the hook ("7 Skills", "5 Rules", "10 Mistakes")

### Good infographic topics:
- "7 Skills to Master AI in 2026"
- "5 Rules for Prompting Claude (That Nobody Follows)"
- "10 AI Automations That Actually Save Time"
- "6 Steps to Build Your First AI Workflow"
- "The 8 Biggest AI Mistakes Businesses Make"
- "4 Things to Set Up Before You Touch Any AI Tool"
- "9 Free AI Tools That Replace Paid Software"

### Bad infographic topics:
- Anything opinion-only with no steps (that's a carousel, not an infographic)
- Anything with fewer than 4 sections (not dense enough to feel valuable)
- Anything too broad ("Everything About AI") — needs a specific angle

### Content Mix (Per Week — 2 Infographics)
- **1 "How-To" infographic** — Steps, tools, process (reference material)
- **1 "Rules/Mistakes" infographic** — Opinionated, framework-based (shareable)

### Step 2: Screenshot with Playwright

```python
from playwright.sync_api import sync_playwright
import time

with sync_playwright() as p:
    browser = p.chromium.launch()
    page = browser.new_page(viewport={'width': 1080, 'height': 1350})
    page.goto('file://' + html_path)
    time.sleep(3)  # Font render wait
    page.screenshot(path='infographic.png', full_page=True)
```

Note: Use `full_page=True` since infographics may exceed 1350px height depending on content density. The PNG output IS the final deliverable (LinkedIn accepts image uploads directly).

# THE 10 NON-NEGOTIABLES

1. **Contrarian hooks only.** If it sounds like common advice, throw it away
2. **Slide 1 = High Tension. Slide 5 = Aha. Slide 9 = Bridge.** Non-negotiable positions
3. **Robot check every line.** If it sounds written, rewrite it until it sounds spoken
4. **Think tank, not content writer.** Strategy and contrarian angle FIRST. Content second
5. **Carousel = content + prompts only.** This skill does NOT design carousels
6. **Infographic = fully designed.** This skill builds, screenshots, and exports infographics
7. **1 carousel + 2 infographics per week.** Consistent cadence
8. **70% visual for carousels.** Simple text = complex image. Complex text = simple image
9. **Every infographic section ends with an italic punchline.** Opinions, not summaries
10. **Repost = generous act.** Always frame the CTA as helping others, never self-promotion
